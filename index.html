William Henry Gates III

This is an article about the co-founder of Microsoft

This is a timeline of Bill Gates' career

  • 1955: Born in Seattle, Washington, U.S.
  • 1967: Gates is enrolled in the Lakeside School, an exclusive private school in Seattle, where he befriends Paul Allen
  • 1973: Gates enters Harvard as a pre-law major
  • 1975: Gates takes his first leave of absence from school to start working on the software venture he refers to as Micro-Soft.
  • 1976: Gates and Allen register the trademark “Microsoft.”
  • 1978: Microsoft’s year-end sales exceed $1 million.
  • 1981: Microsoft incorporates and buys the rights to the operating system “DOS” from Seattle Computer Products. The system is modified and renamed MS-DOS, and the company licenses it to IBM for the company’s new personal computer.
  • 1983: Allen leaves Microsoft after developing Hodgkin’s disease
  • 1987: At age 31, Gates becomes the youngest billionaire ever
  • 1989: Gates founds Corbis, an archive of art and photography from public and private collections
  • 1995: In July, at age 39 and with a fortune of $12.9 billion, Gates becomes the world’s richest man
  • 2000: Gates steps down as CEO of Microsoft but becomes chief software architect.
  • 2005: Queen Elizabeth II bestows an honorary knighthood on Gates for his contributions to the United Kingdom
  • 2007: Gates “graduates” from Harvard; the university awards him with an honorary degree
  • 2014: He stepped down as chairman of Microsoft to become technology advisor at the firm
  • 2020: Microsoft announced Gates would be leaving his board positions at Berkshire Hathaway and Microsoft to dedicate himself to philanthropic endeavors such as climate change, global health and development
